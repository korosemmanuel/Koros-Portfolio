Comparative Assessment of SVM and RF Algorithms For Flood Susceptibility Mapping

Flood Susceptibility Mapping

The project presented a comparative assessment of Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Random Forest (RF) algorithms for flood susceptibility mapping

Project Description:

High-dimensional datasets derived from remote sensing techniques, particularly Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery, alongside traditional geo-environmental datasets were used, the study addresses the challenge of limited flood inventory data in the region. A flood inventory map was developed using SAR images, and critical flood conditioning factors such as soil type, land use, elevation, and rainfall were incorporated into the analysis.

The methodological framework involved collecting and preprocessing multi-source geospatial data, generating a flood inventory through Google Earth Engine and applying both SVM and RF algorithms for flood susceptibility modeling. Spatial flood susceptibility maps were generated, and model performance was evaluated through cross-validation on unseen data to ensure robustness.

Maps:

Flood inventory maps for 2019, 2020, and 2021 were generated using Sentinel-1 SAR data, showing flood extents ranging from 9 km² in 2020 to over 15 km² in 2021, depending on rainfall variability. Flood susceptibility maps were produced using Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Random Forest (RF) models, with lower elevations, gentle slopes, and rainfall identified as the most influential flood conditioning factors. Random Forest achieved a higher overall accuracy (96.49%) compared to SVM (92.27%), as well as lower prediction errors (RMSE and MAE), indicating better model performance and reliability. Both models demonstrated strong capability for flood susceptibility mapping, with RF showing superior consistency and predictive power.

Conclusion:

The study confirmed the effectiveness of Sentinel-1 SAR data for flood mapping, particularly in variable weather conditions. Random Forest outperformed SVM in mapping flood susceptibility, offering higher accuracy and better handling of complex spatial patterns.The Random Forest model proved to be a more reliable and robust tool for flood susceptibility analysis in the study area, supporting its use for future flood risk management and mitigation planning.
