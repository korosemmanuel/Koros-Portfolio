Mapping Urban Growth Using Nighttime Lights Data

This project explores urban expansion in Nairobi by analyzing satellite-derived nightlight data from 2013 and 2021, revealing how the city’s footprint has evolved over time. By integrating nightlight changes with land cover data, we visualize and quantify the spatial patterns of urban growth.

Project Description:

This project focused on the years 2013 and 2021 respectively as shown in below to assess Nairobi’s nightlight intensity changes. These datasets help trace shifts in illuminated areas, providing a unique proxy for population density, economic activity, and built-up expansion.

Maps:

By calculating the pixel-wise difference between 2021 and 2013 nightlight data, we generated an urban growth image highlighting where brightness increased or decreased. Positive changes mapped in green color, red color represents negative change revealing zones of significant expansion.

Conclusion:

This project demonstrates how combining satellite nightlight analysis with land cover mapping can effectively monitor urbanization trends over time. Such insights are invaluable for urban planners, policymakers, and researchers seeking to understand and manage the rapid growth of African cities.
